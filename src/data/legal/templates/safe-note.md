# SAFE (SIMPLE AGREEMENT FOR FUTURE EQUITY)

**Effective Date:** {{Effective Date}}

This Simple Agreement for Future Equity ("SAFE") is entered into by and between:

**{{Company Name}}**, with its principal place of business at {{Company Address}} ("Company"), and

**{{Investor Name}}**, with its principal place of business at {{Investor Address}} ("Investor").

## 1. Events

1.1. **Equity Financing.** If there is an Equity Financing before the termination of this SAFE, upon the initial closing of such Equity Financing, this SAFE will automatically convert into the number of shares of Safe Preferred Stock equal to the Purchase Amount divided by the Conversion Price.

(a) The "Conversion Price" shall be the lesser of: (i) the price per share of the Standard Preferred Stock sold in the Equity Financing multiplied by the Discount Rate; or (ii) the quotient obtained by dividing the Valuation Cap by the Company Capitalization.

(b) In connection with the conversion of this SAFE into shares of Safe Preferred Stock, Investor shall execute and deliver to Company all transaction documents related to the Equity Financing. The Safe Preferred Stock shall have identical rights, privileges, preferences, and restrictions as the Standard Preferred Stock sold in the Equity Financing, except that the Safe Preferred Stock shall have the liquidation preference and conversion price determined by the Conversion Price.

1.2. **Liquidity Event.** If there is a Liquidity Event before the termination of this SAFE, Investor shall, at Investor's option, receive either:

(a) a cash payment equal to the Purchase Amount; or

(b) a number of shares of Common Stock equal to the Purchase Amount divided by the Liquidity Price.

The "Liquidity Price" shall be the lesser of: (i) the quotient obtained by dividing the Valuation Cap by the Liquidity Capitalization; or (ii) the fair market value per share of Common Stock as determined in connection with the Liquidity Event multiplied by the Discount Rate.

1.3. **Dissolution Event.** If there is a Dissolution Event before the termination of this SAFE, Company shall pay Investor an amount equal to the Purchase Amount, subject to the following: (a) payments under this Section 1.3 shall be made from legally available funds; (b) payments under this Section 1.3 shall be made before any distribution to holders of Common Stock; and (c) if the funds available for distribution are insufficient to pay the full Purchase Amount to all SAFE holders, the available funds shall be distributed pro rata among SAFE holders in proportion to their respective Purchase Amounts.

## 2. Definitions

2.1. "Capital Stock" means the capital stock of Company, including Common Stock and Preferred Stock.

2.2. "Company Capitalization" means the sum of: (a) all shares of Capital Stock (on an as-converted basis) issued and outstanding immediately prior to the Equity Financing; and (b) all shares of Capital Stock reserved for issuance under any equity incentive or similar plan of Company, including any increases to such plans in connection with the Equity Financing; but excluding: (i) this SAFE; (ii) other SAFEs; and (iii) convertible promissory notes.

2.3. "Common Stock" means the common stock of Company.

2.4. "Discount Rate" means {{Discount Rate Percent}} percent ({{Discount Rate Percent}}%) (e.g., if the Discount Rate Percent is 80, a share of Standard Preferred Stock sold at $1.00 per share would result in a Conversion Price of $0.80 per share for this SAFE).

2.5. "Dissolution Event" means: (a) a voluntary termination of operations; (b) a general assignment for the benefit of Company's creditors; or (c) any other liquidation, dissolution, or winding up of Company (excluding a Liquidity Event).

2.6. "Equity Financing" means a bona fide transaction or series of transactions with the principal purpose of raising capital, pursuant to which Company issues and sells Preferred Stock at a fixed valuation, including any conversion of a convertible note, with total aggregate gross proceeds of not less than $250,000.

2.7. "Liquidity Capitalization" means the number of shares of Capital Stock (on an as-converted basis) outstanding immediately prior to the Liquidity Event, assuming exercise or conversion of all outstanding options, warrants, and other convertible securities, including shares reserved under any equity incentive plan, but excluding shares issuable upon conversion of this SAFE and other SAFEs.

2.8. "Liquidity Event" means: (a) a Change of Control; or (b) an Initial Public Offering.

2.9. "Change of Control" means: (a) a transaction or series of related transactions in which any person or group acquires more than fifty percent (50%) of the outstanding voting securities of Company; or (b) a sale, lease, exclusive license, or other transfer of all or substantially all of the assets of Company.

2.10. "Initial Public Offering" means the closing of Company's first firm-commitment underwritten initial public offering of Common Stock pursuant to a registration statement filed under the Securities Act.

2.11. "Purchase Amount" means {{Purchase Amount}}.

2.12. "Safe Preferred Stock" means the shares of a series of Preferred Stock issued to Investor upon conversion of this SAFE, having the rights, privileges, preferences, and restrictions identical to the Standard Preferred Stock, except as set forth in Section 1.1.

2.13. "Standard Preferred Stock" means the shares of the series of Preferred Stock issued to investors in the Equity Financing.

2.14. "Valuation Cap" means {{Valuation Cap}}.

## 3. Company Representations

3.1. Company represents and warrants that:

(a) Company is a corporation duly organized, validly existing, and in good standing under the laws of its state of incorporation;

(b) Company has full power and authority to execute and deliver this SAFE, and the execution, delivery, and performance of this SAFE has been duly authorized by all necessary corporate action;

(c) the execution and performance of this SAFE does not conflict with or violate any provision of Company's certificate of incorporation, bylaws, or any material agreement to which Company is a party;

(d) the shares of Safe Preferred Stock issuable upon conversion of this SAFE, when issued in compliance with the terms of this SAFE, will be validly issued, fully paid, and nonassessable, and free of any liens or encumbrances created by Company; and

(e) Company will comply with all applicable federal and state securities laws in connection with the issuance of this SAFE and the shares of Safe Preferred Stock.

## 4. Investor Representations

4.1. Investor represents and warrants that:

(a) Investor has full legal capacity and authority to execute and deliver this SAFE and to perform Investor's obligations hereunder;

(b) Investor is an "accredited investor" as defined in Rule 501 of Regulation D under the Securities Act of 1933, as amended;

(c) Investor is acquiring this SAFE and the securities issuable upon conversion hereof for Investor's own account, for investment purposes only, and not with a view to or for the purpose of resale or distribution;

(d) Investor understands that this SAFE and the securities issuable upon conversion hereof have not been registered under the Securities Act or any state securities laws, and that such securities may not be offered, sold, or transferred except in compliance with applicable securities laws;

(e) Investor understands that an investment in Company involves a high degree of risk, including the risk of total loss of Investor's investment, and Investor has the financial ability to bear such risk; and

(f) Investor has had an opportunity to ask questions of and receive answers from Company regarding the terms and conditions of this SAFE and the business, properties, prospects, and financial condition of Company.

## 5. Miscellaneous

5.1. **No Stockholder Rights.** Investor shall have no rights as a stockholder of Company until and unless this SAFE is converted into shares of Safe Preferred Stock. Prior to conversion, this SAFE shall not entitle Investor to any voting rights, dividend rights, or other rights of a stockholder.

5.2. **Tax Treatment.** The Parties intend that this SAFE shall be treated as equity for U.S. federal and state income tax purposes. The Parties shall not take any position inconsistent with such treatment on any tax return or in any tax proceeding, unless required by applicable law.

5.3. **Security.** This SAFE is not a debt instrument and shall not be construed as creating any indebtedness. Investor shall not have any creditor's rights against Company with respect to this SAFE, except as set forth in Section 1.3 (Dissolution Event).

5.4. **Governing Law.** This SAFE shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of {{Governing Law State}}, without regard to its conflict of laws provisions.

5.5. **Dispute Resolution.** Any dispute arising out of or relating to this SAFE shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in the State of {{Governing Law State}}, and each Party irrevocably consents to the jurisdiction of such courts.

5.6. **Notices.** All notices under this SAFE shall be in writing and shall be deemed given when: (a) delivered personally; (b) sent by confirmed email; or (c) sent by nationally recognized overnight courier, addressed to the Party at the address set forth in this SAFE or such other address as the Party may designate by written notice.

5.7. **Assignment.** Neither Party may assign this SAFE without the prior written consent of the other Party, except that Company may assign this SAFE in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets, provided the assignee assumes Company's obligations hereunder.

5.8. **Severability.** If any provision of this SAFE is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect.

5.9. **Entire Agreement.** This SAFE constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether oral or written.

5.10. **Amendment.** No amendment or modification of this SAFE shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.

5.11. **Waiver.** The failure of either Party to enforce any provision of this SAFE shall not constitute a waiver of such Party's right to enforce that provision or any other provision in the future.

5.12. **Counterparts.** This SAFE may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one instrument.

5.13. **Most Favored Nation.** If Company issues any subsequent SAFE or convertible security with terms more favorable to the investor than those set forth in this SAFE (including a lower valuation cap or higher discount rate), Company shall promptly notify Investor, and Investor may elect to adopt such more favorable terms by providing written notice to Company within fifteen (15) days.
