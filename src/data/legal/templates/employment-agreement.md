# EMPLOYMENT AGREEMENT

This Employment Agreement ("Agreement") is entered into by and between:

**{{Company Name}}**, with its principal place of business at {{Company Address}} ("Company"), and

**{{Employee Name}}**, residing at {{Employee Address}} ("Employee").

Company and Employee are each referred to herein as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties."

## 1. Background

Company desires to employ Employee, and Employee desires to accept employment with Company, subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement.

## 2. Position and Duties

2.1. **Title.** Company shall employ Employee in the position of {{Job Title}}.

2.2. **Start Date.** Employee's employment shall commence on {{Start Date}} (the "Start Date").

2.3. **Duties.** Employee shall perform all duties and responsibilities customarily associated with the position of {{Job Title}}, as well as any additional duties reasonably assigned by Company from time to time. Employee shall devote full professional time, attention, and best efforts to the performance of Employee's duties.

2.4. **Compliance.** Employee shall comply with all Company policies, procedures, and codes of conduct, as may be updated from time to time at Company's discretion.

2.5. **Reporting.** Employee shall report to the individual designated by Company or such other person as Company may designate from time to time.

## 3. Compensation and Benefits

3.1. **Base Salary.** Company shall pay Employee an annual base salary of {{Annual Salary}} (the "Base Salary"), payable in equal installments according to Company's standard payroll schedule, less applicable withholdings and deductions required by law.

3.2. **Benefits.** Employee shall be eligible to participate in Company's standard employee benefit plans and programs, subject to the terms and eligibility requirements of each plan. Company reserves the right to modify, suspend, or terminate any benefit plan at any time in its sole discretion.

3.3. **Paid Time Off.** Employee shall be entitled to paid time off in accordance with Company's standard policies, as may be updated from time to time.

3.4. **Expenses.** Company shall reimburse Employee for reasonable, pre-approved business expenses incurred in the performance of Employee's duties, in accordance with Company's expense reimbursement policy.

## 4. At-Will Employment

4.1. **At-Will Status.** Employee's employment with Company is "at will." Either Company or Employee may terminate the employment relationship at any time, with or without cause, and with or without notice, subject to Section 8 of this Agreement.

4.2. **No Guaranteed Term.** Nothing in this Agreement shall be construed as a guarantee of employment for any specific period of time or as altering the at-will nature of Employee's employment.

## 5. Confidentiality

5.1. **Definition.** "Confidential Information" means any non-public information relating to Company's business, operations, products, services, customers, finances, technology, trade secrets, or other proprietary matters, whether disclosed orally, in writing, electronically, or by observation. Confidential Information includes, without limitation, business plans, customer lists, financial data, pricing information, product roadmaps, source code, algorithms, and personnel information.

5.2. **Obligations.** Employee shall: (a) use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of performing Employee's duties for Company; (b) protect Confidential Information using at least reasonable care; (c) not disclose Confidential Information to any third party without Company's prior written consent; and (d) return all Confidential Information and copies thereof upon termination of employment.

5.3. **Exclusions.** Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of Employee; (b) was known to Employee before employment with Company, as demonstrated by written records; or (c) is required to be disclosed by law, provided Employee gives Company prompt written notice and cooperates with Company's efforts to obtain a protective order.

5.4. **Duration.** The obligations of confidentiality shall survive termination of Employee's employment and continue indefinitely, or for as long as the information remains non-public and qualifies as a trade secret under applicable law.

## 6. Intellectual Property Assignment

6.1. **Assignment.** Employee hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in and to any and all inventions, discoveries, improvements, works of authorship, designs, ideas, and other intellectual property (collectively, "Inventions") that Employee conceives, creates, develops, or reduces to practice, alone or jointly with others, during the period of Employee's employment with Company, and that: (a) relate to Company's current or anticipated business, products, or research; (b) result from any work performed by Employee for Company; or (c) are developed using Company's equipment, resources, or Confidential Information.

6.2. **Disclosure.** Employee shall promptly and fully disclose all Inventions to Company in writing.

6.3. **Further Assurances.** Employee shall execute any documents and take any actions reasonably necessary to perfect Company's ownership of the Inventions, including patent applications, copyright registrations, and assignments.

6.4. **Prior Inventions.** Employee has listed on Exhibit A all inventions, if any, that Employee owns as of the Start Date and wishes to exclude from this Agreement ("Prior Inventions"). If no Exhibit A is attached, Employee represents that no Prior Inventions exist.

6.5. **Work for Hire.** To the extent any Invention qualifies as a "work made for hire" under applicable copyright law, it shall be considered a work made for hire, with Company as the author.

## 7. Non-Solicitation

7.1. **Non-Solicitation of Employees.** During Employee's employment and for a period of twelve (12) months following termination (the "Restricted Period"), Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit, recruit, or hire any employee or contractor of Company, or induce any such person to terminate their relationship with Company.

7.2. **Non-Solicitation of Customers.** During the Restricted Period, Employee shall not, directly or indirectly, solicit or attempt to solicit any customer or client of Company with whom Employee had material contact during the last twelve (12) months of Employee's employment, for the purpose of providing products or services competitive with Company's business.

7.3. **Reasonableness.** Employee acknowledges that the restrictions in this Section 7 are reasonable in scope, duration, and geographic extent, and are necessary to protect Company's legitimate business interests.

## 8. Termination

8.1. **Termination by Company.** Company may terminate Employee's employment at any time, with or without cause, upon written notice to Employee.

8.2. **Termination by Employee.** Employee may terminate employment at any time upon fourteen (14) days' prior written notice to Company. Company may, in its sole discretion, waive the notice period and accelerate the termination date.

8.3. **Effect of Termination.** Upon termination of employment for any reason: (a) Company shall pay Employee all earned but unpaid Base Salary through the date of termination, plus any accrued but unused paid time off, in accordance with applicable law; (b) Employee shall return all Company property, including equipment, documents, keys, and access credentials; and (c) Employee shall comply with the surviving provisions of this Agreement.

8.4. **Survival.** Sections 5, 6, 7, and 9 shall survive termination of Employee's employment.

## 9. General Provisions

9.1. **Governing Law.** This Agreement shall be governed by and construed in accordance with the laws of the State of {{Governing Law State}}, without regard to its conflict of laws provisions.

9.2. **Dispute Resolution.** Any dispute arising out of or relating to this Agreement shall be resolved exclusively in the state or federal courts located in the State of {{Governing Law State}}, and each Party irrevocably consents to the jurisdiction of such courts.

9.3. **Injunctive Relief.** Employee acknowledges that a breach of Sections 5, 6, or 7 may cause irreparable harm to Company for which monetary damages would be inadequate. Accordingly, Company may seek injunctive or other equitable relief in addition to any other remedies available at law or in equity.

9.4. **Notices.** All notices under this Agreement shall be in writing and shall be deemed given when: (a) delivered personally; (b) sent by confirmed email; or (c) sent by nationally recognized overnight courier, addressed to the Party at the address set forth in this Agreement or such other address as the Party may designate by written notice.

9.5. **Assignment.** Employee may not assign this Agreement. Company may assign this Agreement to an affiliate or in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or substantially all of its assets.

9.6. **Severability.** If any provision of this Agreement is held to be invalid, illegal, or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force and effect. The Parties shall negotiate in good faith to replace any invalid provision with a valid provision that achieves the original intent to the greatest extent permitted by law.

9.7. **Entire Agreement.** This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties with respect to the subject matter hereof and supersedes all prior agreements, understandings, negotiations, and discussions, whether oral or written.

9.8. **Amendment.** No amendment or modification of this Agreement shall be effective unless made in writing and signed by both Parties.

9.9. **Waiver.** The failure of either Party to enforce any provision of this Agreement shall not constitute a waiver of such Party's right to enforce that provision or any other provision in the future.

9.10. **Counterparts.** This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, each of which shall be deemed an original, and all of which together shall constitute one agreement.
